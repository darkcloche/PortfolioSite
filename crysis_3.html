Sam Howels - Designer

Crysis 3 - Multiplayer

Designer - PC, Xbox 360, PS3 (2013)

  • During pre-production, I was responsible for the initial design work for the Pinger and VTOL features, both of which made it to the final shipped game. The Pinger (shown below) was a boss AI from singleplayer that was added to multiplayer to add some variety into the core PvP experience, and was designed to require players who acquired one to traverse the environment intelligently rather than turn them into a temporary unstoppable force. Players who were able to jump on top of a piloted Pinger were able to rip out the pilot and commandeer the vehicle, meaning players controlling Pingers had to think about where they positioned themselves in relation to ledges and platforms to avoid other players gaining easy access.



  • The VTOL was another feature that was originally built for singleplayer cinematic use, but ended up being a dynamic focal point for multiplayer levels. Players who were able to jump on board the VTOL were able to take control of its side mounted weaponry, giving them increased firepower at the cost of mobility and vulnerability. Smart players were able to use VTOLs to narrowly escape conflict, take down other players from above, and create their own emergent uses from the versatility an armoured floating platform provides.
