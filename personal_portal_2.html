Sam Howels - Designer

Portal 2

Personal Work - Source Engine Exercise

After Portal 2 was released in 2011, I decided to take a break from CryENGINE development and build my first level in Hammer. This was certainly eye opening coming from a non-BSP engine (big thanks to Francois Roughol for helping me plug level leaks), and ended up being a really interesting exercise on learning how to lead players through interior spaces as opposed to laying out large terrain based levels with libraries of artist authored meshes. Looking back later, more improvements could have been made to elements like lighting (both to aid player navigation and to add some contrast to the overly bright rooms) but I'm pleased with the flow of the puzzle and how the elements are introduced to the player.

An additional personal aim for this project was to create a combination of mechanics that hadn't been exploited in Valve's story levels. This ended up requiring players to float a laser redirection cube through an excursion funnel, and orientating this in a way that would redirect the lasers damage at turrets blocking the player's path. The map went through a few iterations after posting online, responding to user feedback and implementing changes to prevent severe shortcuts through the puzzle. I recorded the playthrough of the map shown below in January 2016. Aided by terrible memory, I had the interesting experience of completely forgetting the solution to my own puzzle and re-solving it as I went.
